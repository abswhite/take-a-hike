Annapurna I Summit

Widely considered The World’s Deadliest Mountain, Annapurna is best suited to the adventurous, brave, and existential type of hiker. You will want to book a mountaineering guide, and climb in a group of 10 to 15 people. It takes 53 days typically (acclimation and climbing), and costs around $20,000.

Memorable quote:

“We slept like a dead and knew nothing. Early morning there came some friends from another camp to congratulate and express their heartfelt condolence. We were in sorrow, we were tired and still we wanted to rescue our casualties but it was not easy and not possible from that point. Aerial rescue was not possible to that height and getting to that area by climbing means putting more lives in danger.”
--Excerpt from Mingma Sherpa’s Annapurna Diary, March 27, 2015

Quick Facts

  • Location: Himalayas
  • Difficulty: Most Difficult
  • Length: 8 miles
  • Elevation: 26,545 ft elevation
  • Hiking Permit: Required ($1,800)

Mortality on Trail

61 total deaths, 191 successful ascents.Annapurna I has the greatest fatality rate among the 14 eight-thousanders. The 34% death rate is followed by 29% for K2 and 21% for Nanga Parbat. Mount Everest is 6.5%.
