West Tiger 3

This is a great “leg burner” hike to do on a nice weekend day. It isn’t particularly easy, but it’s not too hard either. There are a lot of switchbacks on this steady uphill climb to the top. The summit of West Tiger 3 isn’t fancy, but is a good spot to catch your breath and have lunch. Wildlife includes many kinds of birds, deer, and even the occasional bear.

Quick Facts

  • Location: Issaquah Alps, Tiger Mountain
  • Difficulty: Medium
  • Length: 5 miles, round trip
  • Elevation:
    • Gain: 2,1000 ft
    • Highest Point: 2,525 ft
  • Parking Permit: Discover Pass ($10/ car or $30/ year)
  • Trail Permit: None
  • Trail Condition: Good, frequently travelled

Recommended Equipment

  • Backpack
  • Water for the day
  • First-aid kit
  • High-protein food for energy
  • Sunscreen
  • Hiking shoes
  • Hiking poles
